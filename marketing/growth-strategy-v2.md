# Sippy Growth Strategy v2 — Proof-Led, Usage-Led

**Date:** 2026-06-23 · Supersedes the framing in `marketing/twitter-viral-strategy.md` (round 1).
Incorporates Mateo's audit + a second, cited research round (`research evidence brief`, 2026-06-23).

---

## Bottom line

The thesis is right; the danger is letting "go viral on X" and "points for deposits" pull Sippy away from its one unfair asset: **real WhatsApp dollar usage by normal people.** So:

1. **Lead with proof of usage, not deposits/TVL.** Make a live dashboard the hero, not a points leaderboard.
2. **Season 1 is a usage/reputation score, not a token-adjacent points farm.** Reward sends, receives, on/off-ramp, and _active_ referrals — never passive deposits.
3. **Treat Crypto Twitter as credibility distribution, not the customer channel.** X wins builders, investors, KOLs, crypto-native early adopters. LATAM users convert through WhatsApp, trusted communities, events, referrals, simple Spanish.
4. **Resolve the grant tension honestly:** we DO need to move the volume KPI — so we pump it with _real transactions_ (which is what the KPI measures), not mercenary capital that leaves the moment rewards stop.

## 1. Positioning — ride the narrative, cite it precisely

Sippy is the literal embodiment of the reigning institutional thesis. a16z (Chris Dixon) titled it **"The WhatsApp moment for money is here"** (Feb 13, 2026) and, in the April 2025 stablecoins piece, used the exact corridor we serve:

> "Sending $200 from the U.S. to Colombia using traditional methods will cost you $12.13; with stablecoins, it costs $0.01."

Use this anchor — it's confirmed verbatim. **But cite it honestly:** the $0.01 is the on-chain network fee only; a16z's own footnote notes off-ramp fees range 0–5%. So frame it as _"the rails cost a cent; the old rails cost $12.13"_ — not as an all-in end-to-end claim. CT will dunk on imprecision; precision is the credibility play.

Supporting macro to ride (a16z, Feb 13 2026): stablecoins moved **>$12T** after filtering bots/inorganic activity — approaching Visa's ~$17T — and a16z argues users will increasingly just feel like they're "using dollars." We are not pitching a maybe; we're showing the thesis _already working_ in Cartagena.

## 2. The core principle: reward usage, not deposits (the evidence)

Rewards aren't the enemy — **passive/deposit** rewards are. The split is stark.

**Usage-gated rewards amplify a real product:**

- **Hyperliquid — the strongest comp.** Points rewarded real perps trading + referrals _after_ the product already worked, distributed to growing cohorts of actual users; post-airdrop usage held and HLP TVL rose ~$150M → $400M+ into early 2025.
- **Jupiter — eligibility tied to real swap volume**, not idle deposits (first phase ≥$1,000 historical volume), later fee-paying users + active governance. Reward the verb, not the balance.

**Deposit/passive farms collapse:**

- **Blast:** TVL −~65% in 24h post-mainnet ($2.27B → $745M); ~−60% within two months, token ~−91% from ATH.
- **friend.tech:** deposits $52M → $4M; daily fees ~$2M → <$100; new users → single digits.
- **Berachain:** >$1.5B pre-launch deposits → ~$1.1B+ outflows, >70% TVL decline ("ghost chain") as mercenary liquidity left.
- **EigenLayer:** points made it a high-TVL "lockbox" _before_ full functionality — the cautionary tale for points-before-product.

Supporting: the Optimism Airdrop-5 retention study (a16z crypto + Stanford GSB, RDD) found rewarding **genuine engagement raised** 30-day retention while rewarding **raw activity lowered it** — same lesson, cleanly measured.

**Translation for Sippy:** Season 1 _can_ use rewards — this is how we pump the grant's volume KPI — but gate every one on a **real usage verb** (send, receive, on/off-ramp, active referral), pay growing cohorts of real users, and never reward a passive balance. That's the Hyperliquid/Jupiter pattern, not the Blast/Berachain one.

## 3. Season 1 = a usage / reputation score (not a token points farm)

**Design rules:**

- **Score, not points-as-currency.** A non-transferable "Sippy reputation" that _decays_ without continued real use. No public "deposit → points" leaderboard. No token promise (see §6 legal).
- **Reward the verbs that move the KPI:** completed sends, completed receives, on-ramp (`fund.sippy.lat`), off-ramp (COP↔USDC), and **referrals that become active users.** Explicitly **do not** reward passive balance/deposits.
- **Gate every reward on a real milestone, never on signup** — the universal pattern of products that convert:
  - **Grass:** bonus unlocks only after the referee logs **100 hours of real uptime**.
  - **Gemini:** referee must **trade ≥$100 within 30 days**; **Crypto.com** credits after trading milestones (30-day conditions).
  - **Interactive Brokers** (fintech analogue): referrer paid only if the referee **deposits $10,000 and keeps it a year**.
  - **Cash App / Coinbase:** payout only after a real first action (linked card + ≥$5 send; or $100 buy/sell) — never on signup.
- **Referred users are your best users:** peer-reviewed bank study (Van den Bulte et al., _JMR_ 2018) — referred customers had **16–25% higher lifetime value** and durable retention. Two-sided rewards (both parties) is near-universal (Dropbox, PayPal, Cash App).
- **Anti-sybil:** delayed, event-triggered payout (the single biggest fraud control) + device/velocity limits + a proof-of-personhood layer (Human Passport / Coinbase verifications). Phone-number + WhatsApp + a real first send is already a strong sybil gate Sippy has that pure-crypto apps lack.

**The grant tension, resolved:** the M2 KPI is _transacted USDC volume + monthly active wallets_ — not TVL. A loop that pays for **real sends, on-ramps, and active referrals directly generates the exact metric the grant measures**, with users who stay. So we pump the number with the right fuel. (If a separate "deposits" figure is ever shown, present it next to transacted volume — never as the hero.)

## 4. Channels: CT is credibility, WhatsApp is conversion

**CT (credibility distribution):** wins builders, investors, KOLs, crypto-native early adopters, and ecosystem mindshare (Arbitrum, stablecoin thesis). It does **not** convert a Barranquilla freelancer.

**LATAM conversion (the real customer channel) — what actually worked here:**

- **Félix Pago:** US→LATAM remittances **entirely inside WhatsApp**, no app — **>$1B processed, +250k users, NPS >90**, and it scaled by **embedding inside Nubank and Mercado Pago**, reaching Colombia in 2025. This is the closest analog to Sippy and the clearest proof of the channel.
- **Nubank:** ~80–90% of growth from **unpaid referral/word-of-mouth**, CAC ~$5–7 (vs $40+ traditional). Waitlist scarcity + friend-priority.
- **Bitso / Lemon / Belo:** grew via **remittance corridors and cashback**, not paid CT.
- **Corridor size (real demand):** US→Colombia remittances ~**$11.8B** (2024 record, monthly first topped $1B June 2024); US→Mexico **$62.5B**. WhatsApp reaches >90% of internet users in CO/MX.

**So the distribution plan is barbell:** founder-led CT for credibility/capital/KOLs at the top; WhatsApp groups, micro-KOLs, Ethereum Colombia, university/community events, and diaspora corridors for actual users at the base.

## 5. Execution — priority order (Mateo's 7, with the evidence behind each)

1. **Proof sprint.** Ship a public live dashboard: users onboarded via WhatsApp, sends completed, transacted volume, countries, **repeat users**, **retained balances after real use**, active referrals. (We already have `sippy.lat/stats` — extend it with repeat-usage + retention, which are the believable metrics.) This is the asset every thread links to.
2. **Trust sprint.** Tighten custody language to exactly what's true: **"self-custodial smart accounts via Coinbase CDP + on-chain spend permissions; gas sponsored."** Avoid blanket "non-custodial" overclaiming where a spender executes. Precision pre-empts the CT teardown.
3. **Usage loop (Season 1).** Per §3 — reward send/receive/on-ramp/off-ramp/active referrals; dec aying reputation score; no deposit points.
4. **Founder-led CT launch.** One sharp thread, **real numbers not projections**: the Cartagena 141, transacted volume, the $12.13-vs-$0.01-rails framing (honest), the architecture one-liner ("AI understands, never moves money; crypto enforces"), and a link to the live dashboard. Founder voice > brand account on CT.
5. **LATAM distribution.** Micro-KOLs (rev-share with **clear #ad disclosure**, pre-approved claims), Ethereum Colombia (~500), WhatsApp groups, university/community events (Pizza Day format), and **diaspora US→Colombia/Mexico** use cases.
6. **No incentivized posting.** **X banned reward-for-posting / InfoFi apps (Jan 2026)** and revoked their API access; **Kaito/Yaps** is the cautionary tale (token −17%, 157k-member account banned, founder conceded the model "no longer viable"). Do **not** build or buy "post-to-earn." Earn mindshare with substance.
7. **Paid-creator compliance.** Any paid creator must disclose the paid connection **clearly, in the post itself** (FTC Endorsement Guides — buried/bio disclosures are insufficient; liability runs to advertiser + creator + intermediary). For anything token-adjacent, SEC §17(b) anti-touting applies (Kardashian settled $1.26M; never instruct creators to hide payment).

## 6. Legal — corrected and load-bearing

- **The March 2026 SEC/CFTC document is a FINAL rule / interpretation / guidance, effective March 23, 2026** — _not_ "out for comment." (Round 1 mis-stated this.) The caution stands and is sharper because it's final: the SEC airdrop interpretation **does not cover cases where users provide money, goods, services, or other consideration in exchange for the airdropped asset.** → **"Deposit now, maybe token later" is a bad public posture.** A usage/reputation score with no token promise (§3) stays clear of this; run the exact wording past Lina before any public points/airdrop language.
- **X bans paid posting:** X's developer policy says services must **not compensate people for X actions** (posts, reposts, likes, comments, replies). The Jan 2026 InfoFi crackdown — Kaito sunsetting Yaps, its Yapper community banned, KAITO −~17% — is the cautionary case. No post-to-earn, ever.
- **Disclosure:** FTC post-level #ad for paid creators; SEC anti-touting for securities-adjacent promos.

---

## 7. The risks to keep naming

- Mercenary capital / farm dynamics (the whole point of §3 — design them out).
- Over-claiming non-custody or all-in cost savings (the CT teardown vector — §2, §5.2).
- Regulatory: token-promise language colliding with the final SEC airdrop interpretation (§6).
- Channel mismatch: spending the budget on CT engagement instead of WhatsApp conversion.
- Sustainability: any reward must survive its own removal — if usage drops when rewards stop, it wasn't real PMF.
