## Why limits exist

Daily limits protect you. If someone gets access to your WhatsApp, they can't
drain your wallet because Sippy caps how much can be sent in a day. You choose
the cap, and you can change it anytime.

## The two tiers

| Status | Maximum daily limit |
|---|---|
| **Unverified** (no email) | $50 per day |
| **Verified** (email confirmed) | $500 per day |

When you first sign up, your cap is $50. Once you
[verify your email](/docs/using-sippy/recovery-email), you can raise it up to $500.

Your actual daily limit is whatever you set in
[Settings](https://www.sippy.lat/settings) -- it just can't exceed the cap for
your verification tier.

## What counts toward the limit

These sends use your daily limit:

- **WhatsApp sends** -- every transfer you make through the Sippy WhatsApp chat
- **Web wallet "Free gas" sends** -- when Sippy submits the transaction for you

## What doesn't count

- **Web wallet "Direct" sends** -- when you submit the transaction yourself and
  pay the gas fee. Direct mode has no daily cap.

So if you need to send more than your daily limit allows, switch to Direct mode
on the web wallet.

## When does the limit reset?

Your limit resets every 24 hours from midnight UTC. If you spent $40 today,
you'll have your full limit again tomorrow.

## Velocity limits

Sippy also watches for unusual patterns -- like many small sends in quick
succession. If something looks off, Sippy may slow down or pause sends temporarily
as a safety measure. This is rare and usually resolves within minutes. It's
another layer of protection in case your account is compromised.

## How to change your limit

1. Go to [sippy.lat/settings](https://www.sippy.lat/settings)
2. Find **Daily limit**
3. Choose a new amount (up to $50 unverified, or up to $500 verified)
4. Save

Changes take effect immediately.
