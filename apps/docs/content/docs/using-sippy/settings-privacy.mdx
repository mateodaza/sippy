## Where to find Settings

Go to [sippy.lat/settings](https://www.sippy.lat/settings). Everything you can
configure lives on this page.

---

## Daily limit

Choose how much you can send per day through WhatsApp and Free gas mode on the web.
This protects you if someone else accesses your WhatsApp.

- Without a verified email: up to **$50/day**
- With a verified email: up to **$500/day**

You can change it anytime. See [Spending limits](/docs/using-sippy/limits) for
the full details on how limits work.

## Language

Sippy auto-detects your language from your phone number's country code. If you
prefer a different language, you can override it here.

Available languages:
- **English**
- **Spanish**
- **Portuguese**

The setting applies to both WhatsApp messages and the web interface.

## Phone visibility

Control whether your phone number is visible on your profile.

- **Visible** -- people who look up your profile can see your phone number
- **Hidden** -- your phone number is private

This toggle exists so you can decide how much you share. Hidden is the safer
default if you're unsure.

## Email

Add or change the email linked to your account. A verified email unlocks the
$500 daily limit and gives you a recovery path if you lose access to your phone.

See [Recovery email](/docs/using-sippy/recovery-email) for the full walkthrough.

## Wallet backup

You can back up your wallet's private key from Settings. This gives you a personal
copy of the key that controls your funds -- stored by you, not by Sippy.

A few things to know:

- **Keep it somewhere safe.** Anyone with this key can access your wallet. Don't
  share it, screenshot it on a shared device, or store it in an unprotected note.
- **You don't need it for normal use.** Sippy handles transactions for you. The
  backup is there as a safety net -- if Sippy ever shut down or you wanted to
  move your funds to another wallet, this key is how you'd do it.
- **Back it up once.** The key doesn't change. One secure copy is enough.
